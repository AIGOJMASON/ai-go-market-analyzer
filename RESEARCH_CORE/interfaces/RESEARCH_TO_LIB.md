# RESEARCH to LIB Interface

## Purpose

This interface defines the lawful relationship between `RESEARCH_CORE` and `lib/`.

The interface exists so research-related contracts, policies, references, or archived artifacts may be preserved without turning `lib/` into a live research operator.

---

## Interface Rule

`RESEARCH_CORE` performs live intake, screening, trust assignment, and packet emission.

`lib/` preserves canonized documents, references, archives, and indexes.

Live research operations do not move into `lib/`.

---

## What May Cross the Boundary

The following may lawfully move from research surfaces toward library surfaces:

- canonized research contracts
- research policy documents
- archived packet artifacts
- reference materials
- indexes of research artifacts

---

## What May Not Cross the Boundary

The following may not be delegated to `lib/`:

- active intake
- live screening decisions
- trust classification operations
- packet construction
- runtime research orchestration

`lib/` preserves.
`RESEARCH_CORE` operates.

---

## Archival Principle

When research artifacts are archived or indexed in `lib/`, the archive copy does not become the live authority surface that created it.

Authority of origin remains with `RESEARCH_CORE`.

---

## Summary

This interface preserves the distinction between live research operations and library preservation surfaces.
