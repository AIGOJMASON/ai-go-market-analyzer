# RESEARCH to REFINEMENT Interface

## Purpose

This interface defines the lawful handoff from `RESEARCH_CORE` to refinement surfaces.

The handoff exists so research packets can be transformed, analyzed, or interpreted without losing research provenance.

---

## Interface Rule

Only governed research packets may move from `RESEARCH_CORE` into refinement pathways.

Refinement consumers must inherit visible research context rather than receiving detached narrative or reasoning fragments.

---

## What Crosses the Boundary

The lawful handoff artifact is the research packet plus any declared packet metadata needed for refinement.

Core packet elements include:

- source references
- screening status
- trust class
- title
- summary
- scope
- tags

---

## What Does Not Cross the Boundary

This interface does not transfer:

- strategic authority
- execution authority
- canon authority
- continuity ownership

Refinement may reshape or interpret research inputs, but it does not become the source authority that originated them.

---

## Provenance Rule

Any downstream refinement output must remain traceable back to the originating research packet.

Research provenance may not be stripped during transformation.

---

## Summary

This interface exists to let refinement work on governed research inputs while preserving packet origin, trust visibility, and authority separation.
