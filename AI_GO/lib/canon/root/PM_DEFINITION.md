# PM Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of PM within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve PM as the strategic interpretation and propagation authority of the system.

---

## What PM Is

PM is the bounded planning authority that transforms governed upstream signal into strategic direction.

It is responsible for:

- PM refinement
- strategic interpretation
- propagation decisions
- child-core inheritance direction
- lawful downstream planning handoff

PM is not research intake, not trust classification, not runtime ownership, and not direct execution.

---

## PM Responsibilities

PM is responsible for making planning direction explicit.

Its outputs must preserve:

- planning origin
- strategic signal
- propagation status
- target child-core direction when applicable
- bounded inheritance shape

PM turns governed information into lawful planning movement.

---

## PM Boundary

PM must not silently become:

- research authority
- engine authority
- child-core execution authority
- continuity ownership
- monitoring ownership
- runtime boot authority
- canon authorship

Strategic interpretation is not the same as research validation or direct execution.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### Research
Research validates upstream signal. PM interprets that signal strategically.

### Engines
Engine outputs may inform PM interpretation, but engines do not replace PM authority.

### Child Cores
Child cores inherit PM direction and execute domain work, but do not become root planning authority.

### SMI
PM may reflect continuity-relevant planning state into SMI, but continuity remains distinct.

---

## Summary

`PM_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of PM as the strategic interpretation and propagation authority of AI_GO.

PM exists to make planning explicit before downstream inheritance and execution occur.
