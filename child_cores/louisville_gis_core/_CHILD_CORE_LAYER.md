# CHILD_CORE Layer

## Purpose

`child_cores/` is the root-level execution domain layer of AI_GO.

It exists to host declared downstream execution surfaces that receive lawful planning inheritance from `PM_CORE` and perform bounded domain-specific work under inherited authority constraints.

`child_cores/` does not perform raw research intake, trust classification, root strategic ownership, runtime boot orchestration, long-term continuity ownership, or root monitoring ownership.

It is a bounded execution layer.

---

## Authority Role

`child_cores/` is responsible for:

- receiving lawful inheritance handoffs from upstream planning authority
- hosting declared domain-specific execution cores
- preserving the inherited planning origin of downstream work
- operating within bounded domain and output policies
- exposing execution surfaces through declared registry structure

`child_cores/` is not responsible for:

- raw external intake
- research screening
- research trust classification
- PM strategic ownership
- runtime startup control
- continuity ownership
- monitoring ownership
- canon authorship at the root layer

---

## Position in the System

`child_cores/` sits at the root of AI_GO beside other major authorities such as:

- `boot/`
- `core/`
- `RESEARCH_CORE/`
- `PM_CORE/`
- `engines/`
- `lib/`
- `state/`
- `packets/`
- `telemetry/`

This placement is intentional.

Execution cores are a first-class authority surface, not a hidden implementation detail of PM or runtime.

---

## Information Flow

The lawful upstream and downstream flow is:

Governed Research / Refinement  
↓  
PM Strategic Interpretation  
↓  
Inheritance Handoff  
↓  
Child Core Execution  
↓  
Domain Outputs

This layer may execute inherited planning direction, but it may not silently become root planning authority, research authority, or runtime authority.

---

## Internal Structure

The root of `child_cores/` contains the governing documents for child-core execution authority:

- `_CHILD_CORE_LAYER.md`
- `child_core_registry.json`

Each child core may contain its own local identity, inheritance contract, interfaces, output policy, domain policy, registry, and execution subdirectories.

The template child-core surface includes:

- `core_identity.json`
- `inheritance_state/`
- `execution/`
- `outputs/`
- `constraints/`

---

## Boundary Rules

`child_cores/` must preserve the following boundaries:

1. Child cores must receive declared inheritance rather than raw undeclared PM intent.
2. Execution must preserve visible planning provenance.
3. Child-core output must remain bounded by local domain and output policy.
4. Child cores may not silently become root PM or research authorities.
5. Child-core registry membership must be explicit.
6. Child-core state must remain separate from root runtime state, research state, PM state, and library archive state.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### `PM_CORE/`
`PM_CORE/` provides lawful strategic inheritance direction, but does not become the child-core execution layer.

### `RESEARCH_CORE/`
`RESEARCH_CORE/` may influence downstream work through governed research input, but does not directly execute child-core tasks.

### `engines/`
Engine refinement may shape downstream planning or execution inputs, but does not replace child-core execution.

### `core/`
`core/` governs runtime, continuity, and monitoring, but does not directly perform domain execution.

### `lib/`
`lib/` may preserve child-core contracts and archived outputs, but does not perform live child-core execution.

---

## Summary

`child_cores/` is the lawful execution-domain authority layer of AI_GO.

It exists to ensure that downstream domain work is performed by declared execution cores under inherited planning authority while preserving provenance and authority separation.
