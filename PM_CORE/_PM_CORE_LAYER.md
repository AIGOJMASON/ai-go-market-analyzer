# PM_CORE Layer

## Purpose

`PM_CORE/` is the root-level strategic planning authority of AI_GO.

It exists to receive governed downstream inputs from research and refinement surfaces, interpret those inputs as planning-relevant signal, and direct lawful propagation toward child-core execution surfaces.

`PM_CORE/` does not perform raw research intake, trust classification, runtime boot orchestration, long-term continuity ownership, or direct domain execution.

It is a bounded strategic authority.

---

## Authority Role

`PM_CORE/` is responsible for:

- receiving governed downstream inputs
- refining planning-relevant signal
- performing strategic interpretation
- determining propagation direction
- assigning child-core inheritance pathways
- preparing planning outputs for downstream execution surfaces

`PM_CORE/` is not responsible for:

- raw external intake
- research screening
- research trust classification
- runtime startup control
- system continuity ownership
- monitoring ownership
- direct child-core execution
- canon authorship

---

## Position in the System

`PM_CORE/` sits at the root of AI_GO beside other major authorities such as:

- `boot/`
- `core/`
- `RESEARCH_CORE/`
- `engines/`
- `child_cores/`
- `lib/`
- `state/`
- `packets/`
- `telemetry/`

This placement is intentional.

Strategic planning is a first-class authority surface, not a hidden function of runtime or refinement.

---

## Information Flow

The lawful upstream and downstream flow is:

Research Packet / Refinement Output  
↓  
PM Refinement  
↓  
Strategic Interpretation  
↓  
Propagation Decision  
↓  
Child-Core Inheritance / Execution Handoff

This layer may interpret signal into planning direction, but it may not directly execute the child-core work it dispatches.

---

## Internal Structure

The root of `PM_CORE/` contains the governing documents for strategic authority:

- `_PM_CORE_LAYER.md`
- `PM_CORE_IDENTITY.md`
- `CHILD_CORE_INHERITANCE_CONTRACT.md`
- `STRATEGIC_INTERPRETATION_POLICY.md`
- `PM_REGISTRY.json`

The `refinement/` subdirectory contains the operational planning pipeline such as PM refinement, strategic interpretation, propagation rules, and the refinement registry.

---

## Boundary Rules

`PM_CORE/` must preserve the following boundaries:

1. PM may not bypass governed upstream research surfaces.
2. Strategic interpretation must remain explicit, not implied through routing alone.
3. Propagation must not become hidden execution.
4. Child-core inheritance must follow declared contract.
5. PM outputs must remain bounded as planning artifacts, not canon declarations.
6. PM state must remain separate from runtime state, research state, and library archive state.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### `RESEARCH_CORE/`
`RESEARCH_CORE/` validates and emits governed research inputs, but does not convert them into strategic planning direction.

### `core/`
`core/` governs runtime, continuity, and monitoring, but does not replace PM planning authority.

### `engines/`
Engines may contribute interpretive refinement, but do not become PM authority by doing so.

### `child_cores/`
Child cores may receive planning signals from PM, but they remain execution surfaces rather than planning authorities.

### `lib/`
`lib/` may preserve PM contracts and archived outputs, but `PM_CORE/` performs live strategic operations.

---

## Summary

`PM_CORE/` is the lawful strategic interpretation and propagation authority of AI_GO.

It exists to ensure that governed upstream signal becomes bounded planning direction before downstream execution surfaces act on it.
