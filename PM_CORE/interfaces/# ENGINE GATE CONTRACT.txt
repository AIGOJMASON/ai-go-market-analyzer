# ENGINE GATE CONTRACT

## What it is

Governed contract for PM-side refinement engine activation.

## Why it is there

Defines how PM_CORE may invoke bounded refinement engines without granting them
authority over routing, lifecycle, registry state, or continuity truth.

## Core Rule

Engines may refine representation.
They may not redefine authority truth.

## Lawful Invocation Conditions

PM_CORE may invoke an engine only when:

- a PM artifact already exists
- invocation reason is explicit
- allowed transformation scope is declared
- forbidden transformation scope is declared
- engine selection is written to a persisted receipt
- engine output is stored as a separate artifact
- PM retains authority over final routing and handoff

## Allowed Engine Roles

### Curved Mirror
Reasoning refinement only.

May help with:
- interpretive clarity
- structured analytical framing
- bounded candidate reasoning notes
- advisory decomposition of PM summary

May not:
- activate routing on its own
- override domain alignment
- mutate registry truth
- mutate lifecycle state

### Rosetta
Narrative and presentation refinement only.

May help with:
- language shaping
- output polish
- bounded presentation framing
- structured expressive clarity

May not:
- activate routing on its own
- override domain alignment
- mutate registry truth
- mutate lifecycle state

## Required Refinement Receipt Fields

A lawful refinement receipt must include:

- `packet_id`
- `engine_id`
- `invocation_reason`
- `allowed_scope`
- `forbidden_scope`
- `input_artifact_path`
- `output_artifact_path`
- `invoked_at`

## Failure Rule

If refinement fails:

- PM must continue without engine authority transfer
- failure must be persisted truthfully
- routing may continue only from PM-owned truth
