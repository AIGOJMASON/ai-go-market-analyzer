# Strategic Interpretation Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how `PM_CORE` converts governed upstream signal into explicit planning direction.

Strategic interpretation exists to prevent planning from being hidden inside routing, intuition, or implicit downstream assumptions.

---

## Policy Principle

Planning direction must be declared.

No upstream signal may move toward child-core inheritance as strategy unless it has been explicitly interpreted under PM authority.

---

## Strategic Interpretation Inputs

Strategic interpretation may consider:

- governed research packets
- refinement outputs
- declared scope
- relevance to existing child-core surfaces
- propagation feasibility
- constraints visible in the input signal
- planning significance
- downstream inheritance suitability

---

## Strategic Interpretation Outputs

The default strategic outcomes are:

- `propagate`
- `defer`
- `hold`
- `reject`

These outcomes describe planning direction, not domain execution success.

---

## Output Definitions

### `propagate`
Signal is sufficient and relevant for downstream inheritance planning.

### `defer`
Signal may become useful later but should not yet propagate due to missing readiness or insufficient planning confidence.

### `hold`
Signal is relevant enough to retain under PM awareness but not suitable for active downstream inheritance.

### `reject`
Signal is not fit for PM propagation under current scope, relevance, or constraint conditions.

---

## Rules of Interpretation

1. Strategic interpretation must be explicit.
2. PM interpretation occurs after governed upstream validation, not before it.
3. PM interpretation applies to planning direction, not final truth.
4. Signals with unclear downstream fit may not be forced into propagation.
5. Contradictory or unstable planning signal may be held or deferred even when interesting.
6. Failed strategic fit must be marked visibly, not silently routed onward.

---

## Downstream Meaning

### `propagate`
May move toward child-core inheritance and downstream planning handoff.

### `defer`
May remain in PM state or queue for future review.

### `hold`
May remain visible for PM continuity without active propagation.

### `reject`
May not move downstream as valid PM planning basis.

---

## Rationale Requirement

When possible, strategic interpretation should be accompanied by short rationale such as:

- downstream fit
- scope relevance
- constraint conflict
- insufficient planning readiness
- inheritance suitability

This rationale may be stored in PM artifacts or supporting state.

---

## Summary

Strategic interpretation is mandatory inside `PM_CORE`.

Its purpose is to make planning direction explicit before inheritance and downstream execution pathways are activated.
