# ENGINES Layer

## Purpose

`engines/` is the root-level refinement engine layer of AI_GO.

It exists to host declared interpretive engine surfaces that transform governed upstream inputs into bounded refinement outputs for downstream planning and execution authorities.

`engines/` does not perform raw research intake, trust classification, strategic ownership, runtime boot orchestration, long-term continuity ownership, or direct child-core execution.

It is a bounded refinement layer.

---

## Authority Role

`engines/` is responsible for:

- receiving governed upstream inputs through lawful refinement gates
- applying declared engine-specific transformation or interpretation
- producing bounded refinement bundles
- preserving provenance of upstream signal through refinement
- exposing engine surfaces through declared registry structure

`engines/` is not responsible for:

- raw external intake
- research screening
- research trust classification
- PM strategic ownership
- runtime startup control
- system continuity ownership
- monitoring ownership
- direct child-core execution
- canon authorship

---

## Position in the System

`engines/` sits at the root of AI_GO beside other major authorities such as:

- `boot/`
- `core/`
- `RESEARCH_CORE/`
- `PM_CORE/`
- `child_cores/`
- `lib/`
- `state/`
- `packets/`
- `telemetry/`

This placement is intentional.

Refinement engines are a first-class authority surface, not an invisible extension of runtime or PM.

---

## Information Flow

The lawful upstream and downstream flow is:

Governed Research Packet / Declared Upstream Input  
↓  
Refinement Gate  
↓  
Engine Interpretation / Transformation  
↓  
Refinement Bundle  
↓  
PM / Child-Core / Other Declared Consumers

This layer may reshape or interpret governed inputs, but it may not silently become research authority, PM authority, or execution authority.

---

## Internal Structure

The root of `engines/` contains the governing documents for refinement-engine authority:

- `_ENGINES_LAYER.md`
- `REFINEMENT_GATE_CONTRACT.md`
- `REFINEMENT_BUNDLE_CONTRACT.md`
- `REGISTRY.json`

Individual engines such as `curved_mirror/` and `rosetta/` define their own internal policies, prompts, contracts, and registries beneath this root layer.

---

## Boundary Rules

`engines/` must preserve the following boundaries:

1. Engine input must arrive through a declared refinement gate.
2. Refinement must preserve visible provenance to upstream governed input.
3. Engine output must remain bounded as a refinement bundle, not a strategic command.
4. Engine interpretation must not silently become PM planning or child-core execution.
5. Refinement bundles must follow declared contract.
6. Engine state must remain separate from runtime state, research state, PM state, and library archive state.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### `RESEARCH_CORE/`
`RESEARCH_CORE/` validates and emits governed research inputs, but does not perform engine-specific refinement by itself.

### `PM_CORE/`
`PM_CORE/` receives refinement outputs as planning-relevant input, but does not become the engine layer.

### `core/`
`core/` governs runtime, continuity, and monitoring, but does not replace engine interpretation surfaces.

### `child_cores/`
Child cores may consume downstream planning or refinement outputs through declared pathways, but do not replace refinement authority.

### `lib/`
`lib/` may preserve engine contracts and archived outputs, but `engines/` performs live refinement operations.

---

## Summary

`engines/` is the lawful refinement-engine authority layer of AI_GO.

It exists to ensure that governed upstream inputs are transformed through declared gates into bounded refinement bundles before downstream planning or execution authorities act on them.
