# Narrative Refinement Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how the `rosetta` engine performs narrative-oriented refinement on governed upstream inputs.

Narrative refinement exists to extract, clarify, and reshape narrative structure without erasing provenance or exceeding engine authority.

---

## Policy Principle

Narrative refinement must remain bounded.

The engine may transform the structure of a governed input, but it may not silently replace the authority of the source artifact, the refinement gate, or downstream PM strategy.

---

## Allowed Narrative Operations

The Rosetta engine may perform:

- structural clarification
- narrative shaping
- thematic surfacing
- bounded interpretive reframing
- tone and sequence visibility
- uncertainty preservation

These operations are permitted only when tied to a governed input admitted through a lawful refinement gate.

---

## Disallowed Narrative Operations

The Rosetta engine may not perform:

- raw signal intake
- trust assignment
- PM strategic declaration
- child-core execution assignment
- canon authorship
- provenance stripping
- unsupported certainty inflation

---

## Output Rule

Rosetta output must remain a narrative refinement bundle.

It may assist downstream planning or interpretation, but it does not become a strategic command or execution instruction by wording, structure, or implication.

---

## Provenance Rule

Every narrative refinement must remain traceable to:

- the source gate
- the source governed artifact
- the originating upstream authority

Narrative clarity may increase, but source legitimacy may not be overwritten.

---

## Compression Rule

If narrative expansion would increase instability, ungrounded certainty, or unsupported mass, the output should compress rather than elaborate.

Bounded clarity is preferred over speculative extension.

---

## Summary

The Rosetta engine exists to provide lawful narrative refinement.

Its role is to improve structural interpretability while preserving provenance, boundedness, and authority separation.
