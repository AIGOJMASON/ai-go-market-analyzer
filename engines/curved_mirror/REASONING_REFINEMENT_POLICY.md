# Reasoning Refinement Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how the `curved_mirror` engine performs reasoning-oriented refinement on governed upstream inputs.

Reasoning refinement exists to extract, clarify, and reshape reasoning structure without erasing provenance or exceeding engine authority.

---

## Policy Principle

Reasoning refinement must remain bounded.

The engine may transform the structure of a governed input, but it may not silently replace the authority of the source artifact, the refinement gate, or downstream PM strategy.

---

## Allowed Reasoning Operations

The Curved Mirror engine may perform:

- structural clarification
- reasoning-path surfacing
- implication shaping
- bounded interpretive reframing
- conflict visibility
- uncertainty preservation

These operations are permitted only when tied to a governed input admitted through a lawful refinement gate.

---

## Disallowed Reasoning Operations

The Curved Mirror engine may not perform:

- raw signal intake
- trust assignment
- PM strategic declaration
- child-core execution assignment
- canon authorship
- provenance stripping
- unsupported certainty inflation

---

## Output Rule

Curved Mirror output must remain a reasoning refinement bundle.

It may assist downstream planning or interpretation, but it does not become a strategic command or execution instruction by wording, structure, or implication.

---

## Provenance Rule

Every reasoning refinement must remain traceable to:

- the source gate
- the source governed artifact
- the originating upstream authority

Reasoning clarity may increase, but source legitimacy may not be overwritten.

---

## Compression Rule

If reasoning expansion would increase instability, ungrounded certainty, or unsupported mass, the output should compress rather than elaborate.

Bounded clarity is preferred over speculative extension.

---

## Summary

The Curved Mirror engine exists to provide lawful reasoning refinement.

Its role is to improve structural interpretability while preserving provenance, boundedness, and authority separation.
