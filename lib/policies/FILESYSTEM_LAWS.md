# Filesystem Laws

## Purpose

This document preserves the filesystem laws of AI_GO.

Its purpose is to ensure that the system’s folder and file structure reflects real authority boundaries rather than accidental convenience.

---

## Filesystem Principle

The filesystem is part of system governance.

Directory structure is not merely storage layout.
It is a visible expression of authority separation, information flow, and boundary discipline.

---

## Filesystem Laws

### Law 1: Root Authorities Must Be Visible

Major authority surfaces must appear as visible top-level structures or clearly governed subordinate structures.

---

### Law 2: Canon, Runtime, State, Packets, and Archives Must Remain Distinct

The filesystem must preserve separation between:

- canonical documents
- runtime code
- mutable state
- handoff artifacts
- archives and preserved references

These may relate, but they may not collapse into one another.

---

### Law 3: Operational Ownership Must Match Directory Placement

A file’s directory placement must reflect its true authority role.

A runtime file may not masquerade as canon.
A canon file may not masquerade as mutable state.

---

### Law 4: Interfaces and Contracts Must Remain Explicit

Cross-authority movement must be represented by explicit contracts and interface documents rather than hidden coupling.

---

### Law 5: Registries Must Preserve Visibility

Where surfaces are declared, registries should make those declarations visible so authority membership is inspectable.

---

### Law 6: Child Core Structures Must Remain Uniform

Child-core execution surfaces should follow the same governance architecture unless explicitly versioned otherwise.

---

### Law 7: Archival Location Does Not Alter Origin

A document placed in `lib/` for preservation does not lose the identity of its originating authority.

---

### Law 8: Naming Must Preserve Mapping

Filesystem-safe names may differ from canon names, but the mapping must remain explicit and stable.

---

## Summary

`FILESYSTEM_LAWS.md` preserves the structural laws that keep the AI_GO filesystem aligned to real authority, provenance, and governance boundaries.
