# Child Core to SMI Interface

## Purpose

This interface preserves the lawful relationship between child-core execution surfaces and SMI continuity surfaces within AI_GO.

It exists so child-core milestones, unresolved items, and accepted changes may be reflected into continuity without transferring continuity ownership to execution surfaces.

---

## Interface Rule

A child core may emit bounded continuity-relevant artifacts or signals to SMI.

SMI preserves continuity state.
Child cores do not become the continuity layer.

---

## What May Cross the Boundary

The following may lawfully move from child-core execution toward SMI:

- execution milestones
- accepted local state changes
- unresolved domain items
- bounded continuity notes
- output references

---

## What May Not Cross the Boundary

The following may not be delegated to child cores:

- continuity ownership
- unresolved queue ownership
- change-ledger authority
- long-range continuity governance
- cross-system continuity orchestration

---

## Summary

This interface preserves the distinction between local execution work and system continuity governance.
