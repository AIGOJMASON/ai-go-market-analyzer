# Document Lifecycle Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how documents move through the lifecycle within the `lib/` canonical archive.

The lifecycle exists to ensure documents remain traceable, stable, and governed from creation to archival preservation.

---

## Lifecycle Stages

Documents may pass through the following lifecycle states:

1. **Draft**
2. **Registered**
3. **Canonical**
4. **Deprecated**
5. **Archived**

---

## Stage Definitions

### Draft

The document exists but has not yet been formally registered.

Draft documents may change frequently and are not treated as canonical reference.

---

### Registered

The document has been recorded in the document registry.

It is known to the system but may not yet be authoritative.

---

### Canonical

The document has been validated and accepted as an official system reference.

Canonical documents are indexed in the Canon Index and may be referenced by system authorities.

---

### Deprecated

The document has been superseded or replaced but remains preserved for historical traceability.

---

### Archived

The document is preserved purely for historical reference and is no longer active within system governance.

---

## Lifecycle Rules

1. Documents must be registered before becoming canonical.
2. Canonical documents must appear in the Canon Index.
3. Deprecated documents must remain traceable to their replacement.
4. Archived documents may not be reactivated without explicit re-registration.
5. Lifecycle transitions must be recorded in the Lifecycle Index.

---

## Boundary Principle

Document lifecycle state does not alter the authority of the originating system layer.

Lifecycle status reflects document governance, not operational control.

---

## Summary

The document lifecycle policy ensures canonical system knowledge remains structured, traceable, and preserved across time.
