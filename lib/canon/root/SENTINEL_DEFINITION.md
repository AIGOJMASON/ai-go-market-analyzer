# SENTINEL Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of SENTINEL within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve Sentinel as the system-health and drift-monitoring authority of AI_GO.

---

## What SENTINEL Is

SENTINEL is the bounded long-range monitoring surface of the system.

It is responsible for observing:

- drift
- anomaly patterns
- runtime instability
- structural degradation
- cross-layer health signals

Sentinel is not a runtime router, not artifact verification itself, and not continuity ownership.

---

## SENTINEL Responsibilities

SENTINEL is responsible for:

- long-range health observation
- anomaly awareness
- drift detection
- systemic instability awareness
- warning surfaces for structural degradation

Sentinel answers the question:

Is the system remaining healthy and within lawful bounds over time?

---

## SENTINEL Boundary

Sentinel must not silently become:

- Watcher artifact verification
- research authority
- PM authority
- child-core execution authority
- continuity ownership
- boot authority
- canon archive authority

System-health monitoring is not the same as verification, execution, or continuity preservation.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### Watcher
Watcher verifies declared artifacts and events. Sentinel tracks broader health, drift, and anomaly conditions.

### CORE
Sentinel may be implemented under monitoring surfaces coordinated by CORE, but Sentinel remains its own monitoring meaning.

### SMI
Continuity records may contribute health signals, but continuity and monitoring remain separate.

### PM and Child Cores
Planning and execution events may influence Sentinel observations, but they do not change Sentinel’s monitoring role.

---

## Summary

`SENTINEL_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of SENTINEL as the system-health and drift-monitoring authority of AI_GO.

Sentinel exists to observe long-range structural integrity without replacing verification, continuity, planning, or execution.
