# WATCHER Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of WATCHER within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve Watcher as the verification authority of the system.

---

## What WATCHER Is

WATCHER is the bounded artifact-verification and event-confirmation surface of AI_GO.

It is responsible for verifying that declared artifacts, transitions, and handoff events actually occurred.

WATCHER is not runtime orchestration, not continuity ownership, and not system-health interpretation at the long-range level.

---

## WATCHER Responsibilities

WATCHER is responsible for:

- artifact verification
- event confirmation
- transition tracking support
- declared handoff verification
- verification visibility across system flow

Watcher answers the question:

Did the declared thing actually occur?

---

## WATCHER Boundary

WATCHER must not silently become:

- research authority
- PM authority
- child-core execution authority
- continuity ownership
- boot authority
- canon authorship
- long-range systemic health authority

Verification is not the same as strategic interpretation or health diagnosis.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### CORE
Watcher may be implemented under monitoring surfaces within CORE, but verification remains its own authority.

### Sentinel
Sentinel monitors system health and drift over time. Watcher verifies concrete artifacts and transitions.

### PM
PM may expose artifacts to Watcher for verification, but PM does not own verification.

### Child Cores
Child-core artifacts may be verified by Watcher, but child cores do not become monitoring authorities.

---

## Summary

`WATCHER_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of WATCHER as the verification authority of AI_GO.

Watcher exists to confirm artifacts and transitions without replacing system health monitoring, planning, continuity, or execution.
