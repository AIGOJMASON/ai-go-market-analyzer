# RESEARCH Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of RESEARCH within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve Research as the validated intake and packet-emission authority of the system.

---

## What RESEARCH Is

RESEARCH is the bounded authority that transforms incoming signal into governed research structure.

It is responsible for:

- intake
- screening
- trust classification
- research packet construction
- lawful downstream research handoff

Research is not PM strategy, engine refinement ownership, continuity ownership, or direct execution.

---

## RESEARCH Responsibilities

RESEARCH is responsible for ensuring that raw signal does not move downstream without governed structure.

Its outputs must preserve:

- source visibility
- screening state
- trust class
- bounded summary
- lawful packet shape

Research validates signal before downstream interpretation begins.

---

## RESEARCH Boundary

Research must not silently become:

- PM strategy
- engine refinement authority
- child-core execution authority
- runtime boot authority
- continuity ownership
- monitoring ownership
- canon authorship

Validation is not the same as planning or execution.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### PM
PM consumes governed research as planning-relevant signal but does not redefine research authority.

### Engines
Engines may refine governed research input but do not replace the research intake and trust surfaces.

### Child Cores
Child cores may be informed by research through lawful downstream flow but do not receive raw research authority.

### Lib
Research contracts and archives may be preserved in Lib, but live research operations remain in RESEARCH.

---

## Summary

`RESEARCH_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of RESEARCH as the validated intake, trust, and packet-emission authority of AI_GO.

Research exists to ensure incoming signal becomes governed structure before downstream planning or refinement occurs.
