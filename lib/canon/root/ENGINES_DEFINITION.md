# ENGINES Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of ENGINES within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve the engines layer as the bounded refinement authority of the system.

---

## What ENGINES Are

ENGINES are the declared interpretive and transformational surfaces that operate on governed input admitted through lawful refinement gates.

They are responsible for:

- governed refinement entry
- bounded interpretation or transformation
- provenance-preserving output bundles
- engine-specific refinement logic

Engines are not research intake, not PM planning ownership, and not child-core execution ownership.

---

## ENGINES Responsibilities

The engines layer is responsible for ensuring that refinement remains:

- explicit
- provenance-preserving
- contract-bounded
- separable from research and PM
- structured as refinement bundles

Engines improve interpretability or transformation without claiming root strategy.

---

## ENGINES Boundary

Engines must not silently become:

- research authority
- PM authority
- child-core execution authority
- continuity ownership
- monitoring ownership
- runtime boot authority
- canon authorship

Refinement is not the same as validation, planning, or execution.

---

## Internal Engine Diversity

The engines layer may contain multiple engine surfaces with different refinement styles, such as:

- reasoning refinement
- narrative refinement
- other bounded future refinement types

Different engines may differ in method while remaining identical in authority discipline.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### Research
Research provides governed upstream input.

### PM
PM consumes refinement outputs as planning-relevant signal.

### Child Cores
Child cores may be influenced by downstream outputs shaped through engines, but they do not replace refinement authority.

### Lib
Engine contracts and preserved outputs may be archived in Lib.

---

## Summary

`ENGINES_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of ENGINES as the bounded refinement authority layer of AI_GO.

The engines layer exists to transform governed input into lawful refinement bundles without replacing research, PM, or execution.
