# SMI Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of SMI within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve SMI as the continuity authority of the system.

---

## What SMI Is

SMI is the bounded continuity surface of AI_GO.

It is responsible for preserving:

- long-range continuity state
- accepted changes
- unresolved continuity items
- cross-session historical coherence
- continuity visibility across runtime progression

SMI is not runtime itself, not archive alone, and not PM strategy.

---

## SMI Responsibilities

SMI is responsible for:

- continuity state preservation
- change ledger maintenance
- unresolved queue maintenance
- continuity snapshots
- lawful continuity reflection from other authorities

SMI keeps continuity alive across system movement.

---

## SMI Boundary

SMI must not silently become:

- research authority
- PM authority
- watcher authority
- sentinel authority
- runtime boot authority
- child-core execution authority
- canon archive authority

Continuity is not the same as research, planning, or archive preservation.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### CORE
CORE may host runtime continuity surfaces, but SMI remains the continuity authority.

### PM
PM may reflect milestones and unresolved items into SMI, but PM does not own continuity.

### Child Cores
Child cores may contribute continuity-relevant signals, but they do not govern continuity.

### Lib
Lib preserves documents; SMI preserves living continuity state.

### Watcher and Sentinel
Monitoring may observe continuity artifacts, but monitoring is distinct from continuity ownership.

---

## Summary

`SMI_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of SMI as the continuity authority of AI_GO.

SMI exists to maintain cross-session coherence, accepted changes, and unresolved continuity without collapsing into runtime, archive, or planning authority.
