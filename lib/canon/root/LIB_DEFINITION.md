# LIB Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of LIB within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve Lib as the documentation, canon, and archive authority of the system.

---

## What LIB Is

LIB is the bounded preservation layer of AI_GO.

It is responsible for:

- preserving canon
- preserving contracts and policies
- maintaining indexes
- maintaining document lifecycle visibility
- preserving archives and references

Lib is not runtime execution, not research intake, not PM strategy, and not child-core execution.

---

## LIB Responsibilities

Lib is responsible for preserving the meaning and traceability of system documentation across time.

Its role is to ensure that canonical system knowledge is:

- stable
- indexed
- lifecycle-aware
- preserved
- accessible without becoming runtime

---

## LIB Boundary

Lib must not silently become:

- runtime authority
- research authority
- PM authority
- engine authority
- child-core authority
- continuity authority
- monitoring authority

Preservation is not the same as execution.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### Canon Root
Lib contains preserved root definitions such as this document.

### Research and PM
Policies and contracts from Research and PM may be archived in Lib.

### Engines and Child Cores
Engine and child-core contracts may be preserved in Lib.

### SMI
SMI preserves living continuity state. Lib preserves documentary canon and archive state.

---

## Summary

`LIB_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of Lib as the documentation, canon, and archive authority of AI_GO.

Lib exists to preserve system knowledge without becoming the live operational owner of runtime or execution.
