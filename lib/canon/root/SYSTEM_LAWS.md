# System Laws

## Purpose

This document preserves the system-wide governing laws of AI_GO.

It exists to define the cross-authority rules that constrain every layer of the system.

---

## Law 1: Authority Must Be Explicit

No system surface may act with undeclared authority.

Every major role must be named, bounded, and preserved.

---

## Law 2: Information Must Move Lawfully

Information must move through declared contracts, interfaces, and handoff surfaces.

Raw signal may not silently become planning, execution, or canon.

---

## Law 3: Provenance Must Remain Visible

Every important artifact must remain traceable to its source authority and upstream origin.

Interpretation may not erase origin.

---

## Law 4: Runtime, State, Packets, and Canon Must Remain Distinct

The system must preserve separation between:

- runtime behavior
- mutable state
- handoff packets
- preserved canon
- archival records

These are not interchangeable surfaces.

---

## Law 5: No Layer May Silently Become Another

Research may not become PM.
PM may not become execution.
Execution may not become canon.
Monitoring may not become continuity.
Archive may not become runtime.

Convenience does not override structural law.

---

## Law 6: Inheritance Must Be Explicit

Child execution must occur through declared inheritance rather than hidden implied authority.

Planning origin must remain visible in downstream work.

---

## Law 7: Preservation Does Not Equal Ownership

A document preserved in Lib does not transfer the original authority of that document.

Archive location does not redefine origin.

---

## Law 8: Verification and Health Monitoring Must Remain Distinct

Watcher verifies concrete artifacts and transitions.

Sentinel observes long-range health and drift.

These roles must remain separate.

---

## Law 9: Continuity and Archive Must Remain Distinct

SMI preserves living continuity across sessions and changes.

Lib preserves documents and canonical records.

Continuity is not identical to archive.

---

## Law 10: Canon Constrains Implementation

Implementation may evolve, but canon defines preserved meaning.

When implementation drifts from canon, the drift must be corrected or lawfully re-canonized.

---

## Summary

`SYSTEM_LAWS.md` preserves the cross-authority governing laws of AI_GO.

These laws define the structural discipline that keeps the system coherent, bounded, and governable across growth.
