# CHILD_CORE Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of CHILD_CORE within AI_GO.

It exists to preserve child cores as the bounded execution authorities of the system.

---

## What CHILD_CORE Is

A child core is a declared downstream execution organ operating beneath inherited planning authority.

Child cores are responsible for:

- receiving lawful inheritance
- preserving planning provenance
- performing bounded domain work
- generating local domain outputs
- respecting local domain and output policy

A child core is not root PM, not research, not runtime, and not canon.

---

## CHILD_CORE Responsibilities

Child cores are responsible for converting inherited planning into domain execution while remaining:

- bounded
- provenance-preserving
- domain-specific
- non-root-governing
- structurally uniform across child-core architecture

Child cores exist to execute, not to redefine root authority.

---

## CHILD_CORE Boundary

Child cores must not silently become:

- PM strategic authority
- research authority
- runtime boot authority
- continuity ownership
- watcher or sentinel authority
- canon archive authority

Execution is not the same as planning, validation, or preservation.

---

## Structural Principle

Child cores may differ by:

- domain scope
- execution logic
- local outputs
- local constraints

But their governance structure should remain uniform under the child-core template principle.

---

## Relationship to Other Authorities

### PM
PM provides lawful inheritance to child cores.

### Research
Research may influence child-core work through lawful upstream flow.

### Engines
Refinement may shape child-core-relevant downstream artifacts.

### Lib
Child-core contracts and preserved outputs may be archived in Lib.

---

## Summary

`CHILD_CORE_DEFINITION.md` preserves the meaning of child cores as the bounded execution authorities of AI_GO.

Child cores exist to perform inherited domain work without collapsing into root governance.
