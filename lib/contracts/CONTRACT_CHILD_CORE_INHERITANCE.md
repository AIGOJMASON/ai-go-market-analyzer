# Contract Child Core Inheritance

## Purpose

This contract preserves the canonical structure and boundary rules for inheritance handoffs from `PM_CORE` to child-core execution surfaces in AI_GO.

A child-core inheritance artifact is the lawful propagation surface through which PM planning becomes downstream execution context.

---

## Contract Role

The inheritance contract exists to:

- preserve PM origin of planning direction
- identify the target child core
- preserve scope and planning context
- keep planning and execution authority separate
- provide a lawful downstream handoff artifact

A child-core inheritance artifact is not direct PM execution, not research governance, and not canon authorship.

---

## Required Elements

Every inheritance handoff must contain at minimum:

- `inheritance_id`
- `originating_authority`
- `target_child_core`
- `planning_summary`
- `strategic_signal`
- `scope`
- `tags`
- `created_at`
- `inheritance_status`

---

## Contract Rules

1. PM origin must remain explicit.
2. The target child core must be explicit.
3. Planning provenance must remain visible.
4. Inheritance may not silently become execution ownership by PM.
5. Receipt by a child core does not alter the originating planning authority.
6. Inheritance artifacts must remain bounded and traceable.

---

## Invalid Conditions

A child-core inheritance artifact is invalid if:

- required elements are missing
- the target child core is unclear
- PM origin is omitted
- the handoff attempts to bypass planning provenance
- the artifact overstates authority beyond bounded planning propagation

---

## Summary

This contract preserves the lawful handoff shape for downstream child-core inheritance in AI_GO.
